## 1. LLMs 
- Large Language Models (LLMs) are a type of artificial intelligence model that has been trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like language. They are designed to predict the next word in a sentence, which allows them to generate coherent and contextually relevant text. LLMs have been used in various applications, including chatbots, content creation, and language translation.

## 2. Tokenization
- Tokenization is the process of breaking down text into smaller units called tokens. These tokens can be words, subwords, or characters, depending on the tokenization method used. Tokenization is a crucial step in natural language processing (NLP) as it allows models to understand and process text data effectively. Different tokenization techniques include word-level tokenization, subword tokenization (like Byte Pair Encoding), and character-level tokenization.

## 3. Vectorization
- Vectorization is the process of converting text data into numerical vectors that can be processed by machine learning models. This is typically done using techniques like word embeddings (e.g., Word2Vec, GloVe) or more advanced methods like transformer-based embeddings (e.g., BERT, GPT). Vectorization allows models to capture the semantic meaning of words and their relationships in a high-dimensional space, enabling better performance in tasks like text classification, sentiment analysis, and language generation.

## 4. Attention Mechanism
- The attention mechanism is a technique used in neural networks, particularly in transformer models, to allow the model to focus on specific parts of the input when generating output. It works by assigning different weights to different parts of the input data, enabling the model to prioritize relevant information while processing sequences. This mechanism has been instrumental in improving the performance of models in tasks like machine translation, text summarization, and question answering.

## 5. Transformers
- Transformers are a type of neural network architecture that has revolutionized the field of natural language processing. They are based on the attention mechanism and are designed to handle sequential data, such as text, more efficiently than traditional recurrent neural networks (RNNs). Transformers consist of an encoder and a decoder, which work together to process input and generate output. They have been used in various applications, including language modeling, machine translation, and text generation, with models like BERT and GPT being prime examples of transformer-based architectures.


## 6. Self-supervised Learning
- Self-supervised learning is a type of machine learning where the model learns to predict parts of the input data from other parts, without requiring labeled data. This approach allows models to learn from large amounts of unlabeled data, which is often more abundant than labeled data. In natural language processing, self-supervised learning is commonly used in training language models, where the model learns to predict the next word in a sentence or to fill in missing words based on the context. This method has been instrumental in the development of powerful language models like BERT and GPT.

## 7. Few-shot Prompting
- Few-shot prompting is a technique used in natural language processing where a model is given a few examples of a task in the prompt to help it understand and perform the task better. This approach allows models to generalize from a small number of examples, making it useful for tasks where labeled data is scarce. Few-shot prompting can be particularly effective when combined with large language models, as they have been trained on vast amounts of data and can leverage that knowledge to perform well even with limited examples.

## 8. fine-tuning
- Fine-tuning is the process of taking a pre-trained model and further training it on a specific task or dataset to improve its performance on that task. This is often done after the model has been trained on a large corpus of data, allowing it to learn general language patterns and knowledge. Fine-tuning allows the model to adapt to specific domains or tasks, such as sentiment analysis, question answering, or text classification, by adjusting its parameters based on the new data. This technique is commonly used in natural language processing to achieve better results with less training data compared to training a model from scratch.

## 9. Vector Databases
- Vector databases are specialized databases designed to store and manage high-dimensional vector data, which is commonly used in machine learning and artificial intelligence applications. These databases allow for efficient storage, retrieval, and querying of vector data, enabling tasks such as similarity search, clustering, and classification. Vector databases are particularly useful in applications like image recognition, natural language processing, and recommendation systems, where the data is often represented as vectors in a high-dimensional space. They provide optimized algorithms for indexing and searching through large volumes of vector data, making them essential for handling the complexities of modern AI applications.

## 10. Agents
- Agents in artificial intelligence refer to autonomous entities that can perceive their environment, make decisions, and take actions to achieve specific goals. These agents can be simple rule-based systems or complex machine learning models that learn from data and adapt over time. In the context of natural language processing, agents can be used in applications such as chatbots, virtual assistants, and recommendation systems. They are designed to interact with users, understand their needs, and provide relevant responses or actions based on the input they receive. Agents can also be used in multi-agent systems, where multiple agents work together to solve complex problems or achieve shared objectives.

## 11. RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique in natural language processing that combines retrieval-based methods with generative models to improve the quality and relevance of generated text. In RAG, a retrieval component is used to fetch relevant information from a large corpus of data based on the input query, and this retrieved information is then used to guide the generation process of a language model. This approach allows the model to generate more accurate and contextually relevant responses by leveraging external knowledge, making it particularly effective for tasks like question answering, summarization, and dialogue systems.

## 12. Context Engineering
- Context engineering is the process of designing and optimizing the context in which a language model operates to improve its performance on specific tasks. This involves carefully crafting the input prompts, selecting relevant information, and structuring the data in a way that allows the model to understand and generate more accurate responses. Context engineering can include techniques such as providing additional background information, using specific formatting, or incorporating relevant examples to guide the model's understanding. By optimizing the context, engineers can enhance the model's ability to generate coherent and relevant outputs, particularly in complex tasks that require a deep understanding of the input data.


## 13. Model context Protocol
- Model Context Protocol (MCP) is a framework for defining and managing the context in which a language model operates. It provides guidelines for structuring input data, specifying relevant information, and optimizing the interaction between the model and its environment. MCP helps ensure that the model receives the necessary context to generate accurate and relevant responses, particularly in complex tasks that require a deep understanding of the input data. By following MCP, engineers can improve the performance of language models and enhance their ability to handle a wide range of applications effectively.

- it consists of three main components:
    - mcp client: This component is responsible for managing the interaction between the user and the model. It handles the input prompts, retrieves relevant information, and structures the data in a way that allows the model to understand and generate accurate responses.
    - mcp server: This component is responsible for processing the input data, managing the context, and generating responses based on the model's understanding of the input. It ensures that the model receives the necessary context to generate accurate and relevant outputs.


## 14. Reinforcement Learning with Human Feedback (RLHF)
- Reinforcement Learning with Human Feedback (RLHF) is a technique in machine learning where a model learns to make decisions based on feedback from human evaluators. In this approach, the model receives rewards or penalties based on the quality of its outputs, as judged by human feedback. This method allows the model to learn from human preferences and improve its performance on tasks that may be difficult to define with traditional supervised learning. RLHF is particularly useful in natural language processing applications, such as chatbots and virtual assistants, where the quality of responses can be subjective and may require human judgment to evaluate effectively. By incorporating human feedback into the learning process, models can better align their outputs with user expectations and preferences.

## 15. chain of thought prompting
- Chain of Thought Prompting is a technique in natural language processing where a model is guided through a series of logical steps or reasoning processes to arrive at a final answer. This approach helps the model to break down complex problems into smaller, more manageable parts, allowing it to generate more accurate and coherent responses. By prompting the model to think through the problem step by step, it can better understand the underlying logic and relationships between different pieces of information, leading to improved performance on tasks that require reasoning and critical thinking. Chain of Thought Prompting is particularly effective in applications such as question answering, problem-solving, and decision-making tasks.

## 16. Reasoning Models 
- Reasoning models are a type of artificial intelligence model designed to perform logical reasoning and problem-solving tasks. These models are trained to understand and manipulate information in a way that allows them to draw conclusions, make inferences, and solve complex problems. Reasoning models can be used in various applications, including natural language processing, computer vision, and decision-making systems. They often incorporate techniques such as symbolic reasoning, probabilistic reasoning, and neural networks to achieve their goals. By enabling machines to reason effectively, these models can enhance the capabilities of AI systems and enable them to tackle a wider range of tasks with greater accuracy and efficiency.

## 17. Multimodal Models
- Multimodal models are a type of artificial intelligence model that can process and understand multiple types of data, such as text, images, audio, and video. These models are designed to integrate information from different modalities to generate more comprehensive and accurate outputs. For example, a multimodal model could analyze both the text and images in a social media post to determine the sentiment or context of the content. By combining information from various sources, multimodal models can enhance the performance of AI systems in tasks like image captioning, video analysis, and cross-modal retrieval.


## 18. Quantization
- Quantization is a technique used in machine learning to reduce the precision of the numbers used in a model, which can help to decrease the model's size and improve its inference speed. This is typically done by converting floating-point numbers to lower-precision formats, such as 8-bit integers. Quantization can be particularly beneficial for deploying models on resource-constrained devices, such as mobile phones or embedded systems, where memory and computational power are limited. By reducing the precision of the model's parameters, quantization can help to make AI applications more efficient and accessible without significantly compromising performance.


## 19. Distillation
- Distillation is a technique in machine learning where a smaller, more efficient model (the "student") is trained to replicate the behavior of a larger, more complex model (the "teacher"). The student model learns from the outputs of the teacher model, which can include both the predicted probabilities and the intermediate representations. This process allows the student model to capture the knowledge and performance of the teacher model while being more lightweight and faster to deploy. Distillation is often used to create models that can run on resource-constrained devices or to improve inference speed without sacrificing too much accuracy.

## 20. Small language models (SLMs)
- Small Language Models (SLMs) are a type of artificial intelligence model that is designed to be more lightweight and efficient than larger language models (LLMs). SLMs typically have fewer parameters and require less computational resources, making them suitable for deployment on devices with limited processing power, such as mobile phones or embedded systems. Despite their smaller size, SLMs can still perform a wide range of natural language processing tasks, such as text classification, sentiment analysis, and language generation. They are often used in applications where real-time performance is crucial or where the computational resources are limited.
